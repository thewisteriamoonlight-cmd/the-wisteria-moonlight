The most elegant perfumes are the ones you notice on someone after they have
already left the room. They are quiet, warm, and unmistakably personal.

## Soft floral

Not the loud florals of a department store counter, but powdery iris, tender
rose, a whisper of neroli. These are perfumes for slow mornings — worn to
nowhere in particular.

## Warm musk

A little skin-like, a little cashmere-like — the kind of scent that feels like
a soft sweater in November. Musk perfumes reward restraint; one small dab at
the collarbone is enough.

## Clean citrus

Bergamot, green tea, a distant echo of vetiver. For the days you want to
smell like light itself.

## A note on longevity

The most elegant perfumes rarely announce their presence for the whole day.
They soften, they warm, they become part of you. That is the point.
