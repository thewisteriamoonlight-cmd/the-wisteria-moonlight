Autumn arrives in a home the same way it arrives outdoors: quietly, one small
change at a time. You do not need to redecorate — you only need to shift the
temperature of a few small things.

## Warm the light

Switch to lower-wattage bulbs. Move a table lamp closer to a reading chair.
Light one candle each evening at the same hour. The eye reads warmer light as
a change of season before it reads anything else.

## Warm the textures

A wool throw folded over a chair. A heavier napkin on the table. A linen
runner traded for something softer, in a colour a few shades deeper than
summer's.

## Bring the outdoors in

A single branch from a walk. A small bowl of quinces. A sprig of eucalyptus
on the bedside table. Nothing arranged too carefully — autumn is the season
of gentle imperfection.

## Slow the evenings

Dim the overhead lights an hour earlier. Make tea instead of coffee after
dinner. Read one more page than you meant to. That, more than any decor
change, is what makes a home feel like autumn.
