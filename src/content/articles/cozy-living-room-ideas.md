There is a quiet difference between a room that is decorated and a room that is
lived in. The living rooms we return to — the ones that feel effortlessly
elegant without ever announcing themselves — are almost always the second kind.

## Begin with the light

The most beautiful living rooms are lit softly and from several sources: a
table lamp on a low console, a floor lamp behind a reading chair, a candle on
the coffee table at dusk. Overhead lighting is used sparingly, if at all.

## Layer texture, not colour

A restrained palette does not have to feel cold. Linen upholstery, a bouclé
throw folded over an armrest, a wool rug underfoot, a stoneware vessel on the
mantel — the interest comes from what the eye can *feel*, not what it can
count.

## Leave space for stillness

A truly cozy living room has a place where nothing happens: a corner of the
sofa reserved for reading, an empty section of the coffee table for a cup that
hasn't been set down yet. Restraint is the quietest luxury.
