Paris is a city that rewards sitting still. The right café, on the right
morning, becomes an entire afternoon — and sometimes, an entire memory of
the trip.

## The Left Bank quiet ones

Tucked behind the Sorbonne and along the smaller streets of Saint-Germain,
there are cafés where the marble tabletops are older than most of the
neighbourhood — and where nobody will rush you. Bring a book you have been
meaning to finish.

## The 3rd arrondissement newcomers

A newer generation of cafés in the Marais serves single-origin coffee in
handmade ceramics. The pastry cases are smaller, the lighting is softer, and
the playlists are the kind you want to shazam.

## Sit near a window

The most beautiful part of any Paris café is not on the menu. It is the way
the light comes through the window at eleven in the morning, catching the
edge of a cup and the corner of someone's notebook.

## A note on time

The most beautiful café in Paris is almost always the one you happened to sit
down in when your feet were tired. Do not over-plan.
