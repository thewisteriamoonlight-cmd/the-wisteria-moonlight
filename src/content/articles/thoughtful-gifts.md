The best gifts are the ones the recipient keeps reaching for six months later
without remembering they were given at all. Usefulness, when done beautifully,
is the highest form of thoughtfulness.

## For the reader

A really good bookmark — brass, leather, or ribbon; nothing plastic. A small
reading light that clips to a page. A subscription to one, very carefully
chosen literary magazine.

## For the host

A pair of linen napkins in a colour that suits their table. A ceramic butter
dish. A candle in a scent quiet enough to burn while eating.

## For the traveller

A soft eye mask. A small leather passport sleeve. A refillable travel
atomiser so they can bring their perfume anywhere.

## For anyone

Something to write in. Something to write with. Something to keep them warm.
The three quiet essentials — and almost always the ones people forget to buy
for themselves.
