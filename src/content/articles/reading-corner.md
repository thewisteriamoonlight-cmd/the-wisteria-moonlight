A reading corner is less a piece of furniture arrangement than a small
architectural promise: *this is where I slow down.* Almost every home has a
place that could become one.

## Choose the chair first

The chair sets the tone. It should be soft enough to sink into but structured
enough that you don't fall asleep in it by accident. Second-hand upholstered
chairs, gently reworked, are often better than anything new.

## Add exactly three things

A small side table for a cup. A lamp positioned just behind your shoulder. A
soft throw within arm's reach. That is enough. Reading corners suffer more
from over-styling than from under-styling.

## Face something worth looking at

A window, a bookshelf, a piece of art you love. Never a television.
